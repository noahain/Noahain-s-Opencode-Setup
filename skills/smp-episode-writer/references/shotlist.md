# Shot List Reference — Minecraft Cinematography

## Shot Sizes
| Size | Code | Description | Use Case |
|------|------|-------------|----------|
| Extreme Wide Shot | EWS | Tiny subject in massive environment | Establishing locations, scale, isolation |
| Wide Shot | WS | Full character visible with environment | Action, geography, establishing |
| Medium Wide | MW | Character from knees up | Walking, two-person scenes |
| Medium Shot | MS | Waist up | Dialogue, neutral scenes |
| Medium Close-Up | MCU | Chest up | Emotionally involved dialogue |
| Close-Up | CU | Face only | Emotional peak, reaction, reveal |
| Extreme Close-Up | ECU | One feature (eyes, hands, item) | Tension, detail, emphasis |

## Camera Angles
| Angle | Effect | SMP Use Case |
|-------|--------|--------------|
| Eye-level | Neutral, equal | Standard dialogue, normal situations |
| Low angle | Subject looks powerful/threatening | Antagonist reveal, hero moment |
| High angle | Subject looks small/vulnerable | Character at low point, being watched |
| Bird's eye / Top-down | God perspective, overwhelm | Battle overview, large builds |
| Dutch angle (tilted) | Unease, wrongness, danger | Betrayal, horror moment, instability |
| Over-the-shoulder (OTS) | POV intimacy | Dialogue where one character has power |

## Camera Movements
| Movement | Description | SMP Use Case |
|----------|-------------|--------------|
| Static | No movement | Dialogue, tension, contemplation |
| Pan | Horizontal rotation from fixed point | Revealing a location, following action |
| Tilt | Vertical rotation from fixed point | Revealing height of build, looking up |
| Dolly in | Camera moves toward subject | Intensity increasing, emotional focus |
| Dolly out | Camera moves away from subject | Isolation, reveal of surroundings |
| Tracking shot | Camera follows subject laterally | Walking scenes, pursuit |
| Handheld | Unstabilized, shaky | Chaos, panic, urgency, combat |
| Steadicam | Smooth follow | Character walking with purpose |
| Crane/Boom | Starts high, moves down (or reverse) | Epic establishing, end-of-scene pullback |
| Rack Focus | Focus shifts from foreground to background | Revealing something important behind |
| Zoom | Lens zooms in/out | Dramatic emphasis (use sparingly) |

## Replay Mod Shot Types
- **[REPLAY]** — Camera path recorded after the session via Replay Mod timeline. Best for establishing shots, slow pans, precise cinematic moves.
- **[LIVE]** — Must be captured during the actual recording session. Required for anything with player dialogue and real-time action.
- **[FREECAM]** — Captured live using Freecam mod during session. Good for detached dramatic angles during events.

## Scene-to-Shot Mapping (Common Patterns)

### Dialogue Scene (2 characters)
1. Wide establishing shot of location [REPLAY]
2. Over-the-shoulder on Character A speaking [LIVE or REPLAY]
3. Over-the-shoulder on Character B reacting [LIVE or REPLAY]
4. Close-up on the emotional peak of the scene [REPLAY]
5. Wide pullback as scene ends [REPLAY]

### Action / Combat Scene
1. Wide shot establishing the space [REPLAY]
2. Medium shots of each combatant [REPLAY or LIVE]
3. Handheld tracking during the fight [LIVE]
4. Close-up on hit/reaction [REPLAY]
5. Low angle on the winner standing [REPLAY]

### Reveal / Discovery
1. Character approaches — medium tracking [LIVE]
2. Rack focus: character in foreground, revealed thing in background [REPLAY]
3. Extreme close-up on character's eyes/face [REPLAY]
4. Wide reverse: what they're seeing [REPLAY]

### Emotional / Quiet Scene
1. Wide shot of environment — silence [REPLAY]
2. Slow dolly in on character [REPLAY]
3. Close-up on face during key line [REPLAY]
4. Static medium shot during dialogue [LIVE]

## Visual Storytelling Rules for Minecraft SMP
- Use wide shots to open NEW locations — never cut into a new place on a close-up.
- Save Dutch angles for exactly one moment per episode — they lose power if overused.
- The longer you hold a static shot, the more tension you create.
- Low angle + slow dolly in = "this character just became a threat."
- Replay Mod's camera path keyframes should ease in and ease out — never linear.
- Drone-style bird's eye shots work great for large builds and battle overviews.
- For emotional dialogue: shoot wide first, dolly in through the conversation, end on a close-up at the peak line.
